Byakko

The Jade Stoa (Unreal)

  • Disclaimer: Unreal fights are the same as their Extreme versions but synced up to the current max level of the expansion. But even if they're the same fight, as the years go by, sometimes, the pf standard strats and resolutions can change. That usually involves ignoring a mechanic by resolving it with Tank LB as well as other methods. Have in mind that in order to unlock these fights, you only ever need to beat its normal mode and have unlocked Faux Hollows in Idyllshire.

    • # Phase 1

      Storm Pulse

      • Unavoidable raid-wide

      Heavenly Strike

      • Tank Buster (mitigate it)

      State of Shock

        Step 1

        • MT will be grabbed by Byakko, and OT immediately provokes it.
        • G1 meets South in the stack marker, while MT will be thrown inside it.

        Step 2

        • OT will be grabbed by Byakko, and MT immediately provokes it.
        • G2 meets North in the stack marker, while OT will be thrown inside it.

      Unrelenting Anguish

      • Byakko jumps to the middle and casts orbs, just dodge everything.
      • -
      • At the same time, a tank, healer and dps will be marked with Ominous Wind, a debuff (red orb around the player) that will explode if two (or more) players with the debuff move close to each other.
      • -
      • After targeting a random player, Fire & Lightning: a massive column AoE will be charged up and fired from Byakko during Unrelenting Anguish mechanic (and various other moments during the battle). To avoid, move out of the large red telegraph as quickly as possible.
      • -

      Hakutei (Tiger)

      • Tiger add spawns, OT takes it south because it cleaves, and party focuses it

      Aramata

      • Two random players get targeted with puddles thrice, drop them away enough from people

      Storm Pulse

      • Unavoidable raid-wide

      Distant Clap

      • Party goes in, OT gets targeted with a proximity AOE

      Fire & Lightning

      • Massive randomly targeted column AOE

      Roar of Thunder

      • A very hard hitting AOE that will kill the party unless the add has low enough HP. (Mit this)

        • Aramata Orbs

          These orbs will be coming from clock spots towards the add, block them, each party member should naturally block them from touching the add if standing on designated clock spots.
        • -
        • Note: These orbs used to steal TP from the player before Shadowbringers. Now they inflict a ~3 sec physical damage reduction. So deal with this as you will. You can either have non-physical damage dealers grab all orbs, or keep everyone in their spots blocking their own orb. The debuff doesn't last long enough to warrant such a change, plus it doesn't happen during a burst. In general this won't even be talked about in pf, but rarely it might be brought up.

    # Free Fall

    • After being launched into the air, the raid will begin free-falling. No actions (e.g. attacks or healing) can be performed at this time. During the phase, the outer ring of the arena will kill any player who touches it. Additionally, Byakko will begin firing orbs from far below which players must avoid. Periodically, Byakko will leap up and cast a doughnut AoE which must be avoided by moving into the center. Lastly, Hakutei (the White Tiger) will occasionally cast a straight line AoE through the center, forcing players to move out.
    • -
    • Should you survive the onslaught, the raid will land (stunned) in a new arena be blasted with a massive unavoidable AoE attack from Byakko, killing any player who took too much damage during the free-fall.
    • -
    • Make sure to mit the raid-wide as you fall.

    # Phase 2

      Heavenly Strike

      • Tank Buster (mitigate it)

      Hundredfold Havoc

      • A set of outward-firing AoE lightning patterns that are telegraphed by an arrow icon, displaying the direction they will travel from Byakko (arena center). Two patterns will be fired simultaneously (the second being slightly delayed), giving players enough time for the first pattern to fire outward so that they can move into the now safe area to avoid the delayed pattern.

      State of Shock

        Step 1

        • MT will be grabbed by Byakko, and OT immediately provokes it.
        • G1 meets South in the stack marker, while MT will be thrown inside it.

        Step 2

        • OT will be grabbed by Byakko, and MT immediately provokes it.
        • G2 meets North in the stack marker, while OT will be thrown inside it.
        • -
        • Ideally the MT stays in front of the boss a little longer in order to bait the upcoming Sweep Of The Leg away from the party, thus not making anyone move. Once the cast starts, the MT can immediately head back behind the boss with the rest of the group.

      Sweep Of The Leg

      • This cast begins immediately after the previous State of Shock is resolved. It is a conal AOE that leaves a safe cone right behind the boss (use the hole in the hitbox as a safe area).

      Unrelenting Anguish

      • Byakko jumps to the middle and casts orbs, just dodge everything.
      • -
      • At the same time, a tank, healer and dps will be marked with this time Gale Force, a growing puddle that needs to be dropped on A - DPS | B - Healer | C - Tank. Designated as such because it is color coded with the same colors as the roles.
      • -
      • Afterwards, once again, a tank, healer and dps will be marked with Ominous Wind, a debuff (red orb around the player) that will explode if two (or more) players with the debuff move close to each other.
      • -

      Fire & Lightning

      • Will be cast from the edge of the arena while players are dodging Anguish Orbs and Gale Force Puddles. If the Gale Force puddles have been appropriately placed, players can quickly move to a safe spot between them to avoid the last remaining Anguish Orbs and the oncoming column AoE.
      • -
      • NOTE: From this point onward, a collective set of previously seen mechanics will be executed in sequence. Assuming the group survives the ordeal, Byakko will enter a mechanical loop.

      Hakutei (Tiger)

      • Similar to phase 1, the OT will need to pick up the White Tiger while the MT pulls Byakko to the outer edges. The mechanics are identical (with a few extra overlaps), such as Fire & Lightning (column AoE), Distant Clap (doughnut AoE from Byakko), the proximity marker on the OT, and Heavenly Strike (tank buster).

      Roar of Thunder

      • Will be cast after the above string of mechanics, causing the White Tiger to jump into the center and begin charging an unavoidable AoE with the damage based on its current health (just like phase 1, including the presence of Aramata Orbs). Additionally, the group will have to deal with mechanics from Byakko, such as Storm Pulse, while focusing Byakko and ignoring the add and the orbs. Once the cast reaches around 80% the whole party mits and the tank LB3s.
      • -
      • Fire & Lightning will be cast shortly afterwards, followed by two Storm Pulse casts back-to-back.

      Hundredfold Havoc

      • Will be cast once more, except now it will stack with Gale Force (puddles) which should be placed the same way as they were before. Remember, should a puddle collide with another, the raid will wipe.

      State of Shock

      • Once again we get tanks being grabbed and groups going to stack markers, followed by Sweep Of The Leg.
      • -
      • NOTE: Byakko will now combo various mechanics on a loop (such as Unrelenting Anguish and Gale Force) until he is defeated or he hits the enrage timer (12 minutes). Should the fight go on for that long, he will repeatedly cast Storm Pulse three times before charging up a forced raid-wipe with a fourth cast.
